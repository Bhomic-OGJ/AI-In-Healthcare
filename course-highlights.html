Course Highlights & Key Information

Everything you need to know about the AI in Health Sciences Certificate Program

Eligibility Criteria

Bachelor's Degree from a recognized University, at least equivalent to National Higher Education Qualifications Framework (NHEQF) Level 5.5, with recommended basic proficiency in Mathematics.

Required Disciplines:

Computer Science / Information Technology
Engineering
Mathematics / Statistics
Biotechnology
Bioinformatics
Medicine (MBBS), Nursing, or Pharmacy
All areas of Life Sciences / Pharmacy

Course Applicability

This course is highly applicable to students and professionals seeking to bridge computational techniques with real-world health challenges.

Healthcare Focus

Diagnostics, prognosis, imaging, drug discovery, and clinical NLP

Multi-Source Data

EHR, medical images, genomic profiles, and clinical text

AI Frameworks

State-of-the-art tools, deep learning models, and bioinformatics

Interdisciplinary

Suitable for Computer Science, Engineering, Life Sciences, Medicine

Ethical Focus

Ethical, regulatory, and societal considerations

Course Learning Outcomes

On completing this course, students will be able to:

Identify and describe role, opportunities, and limitations of AI in various healthcare applications
Acquire, preprocess, and integrate multi-source healthcare data
Apply machine learning and deep learning techniques for medical datasets
Develop and evaluate medical AI models using Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch
Design end-to-end pipelines for medical image analysis using CNNs and U-Net
Use AI tools in bioinformatics for biomarker and pathway analysis
Implement NLP pipelines for EHR mining and clinical decision support
Assess AI models for ethical compliance, explainability, and fairness
Analyze legal, regulatory, and societal implications of AI in healthcare
Collaborate effectively across medical and computational disciplines
